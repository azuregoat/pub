Quick Expense Rules of Thumb:

50% Rule: Total operating expenses (not including mortgage) often run about 50% of gross rent. If your rent is $2,000/mo, budget $1,000/mo for expenses.

1% Rule: Monthly rent should be at least 1% of purchase price. A $300K property should rent for $3,000/mo to be a solid investment.

CapEx Reserve: Save 5-10% of rent for big-ticket replacements (roof every 20 yrs, HVAC every 15 yrs, appliances every 10 yrs).

Property Management: Even if self-managing, budget 8-10% of rent so your numbers work if you hire a manager later.

Key Investment Metrics
Glossary & Learning Guide

Cap Rate (Capitalization Rate) — Net Operating Income / Property Value. Tells you the return if you paid all cash. A 5-10% cap rate is typical; higher = more return but often more risk.

Cash-on-Cash Return — Annual pre-tax cash flow / Total cash invested. This is YOUR actual return on the money you put in. Above 8% is good; above 12% is excellent.

NOI (Net Operating Income) — Gross income minus all operating expenses (but NOT mortgage payments). Used to calculate cap rate and property value.

DSCR (Debt Service Coverage Ratio) — NOI / Annual Debt Payments. Banks want this above 1.25. Below 1.0 means the property doesn't generate enough to cover the mortgage.

GRM (Gross Rent Multiplier) — Purchase Price / Annual Gross Rent. Lower is better. Under 15 is typically considered good for investment properties.

The 1% Rule — Monthly rent should be at least 1% of purchase price. Quick filter to see if a deal is worth deeper analysis.

The 50% Rule — Expect about 50% of rental income to go toward expenses (not including mortgage). Useful for quick estimates.

Break-Even Ratio — (Operating Expenses + Debt Service) / Gross Income. Below 85% is healthy; above 100% means you're losing money.
